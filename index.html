Perfect Blue

Perfect Blue is a psychological thriller anime film directed by Satoshi Kon. Released in 1997, it follows a former pop idol, Mima, who transitions to acting, only to face a disturbing breakdown between reality and delusion as she's stalked by a fan and haunted by her past persona. The film explores themes of identity, fame, and mental instability.

Should you watch this movie?

You should watch Perfect Blue if you're a fan of intense psychological thrillers that explore the dark side of fame and identity. Its unique animation style, combined with a gripping plot, keeps you questioning reality, making it a thought-provoking experience. Fans of complex, mind-bending stories will appreciate its commentary on media, obsession, and the pressures of celebrity life.

However, if you're sensitive to disturbing or violent content, Perfect Blue may not be for you. It contains unsettling themes, psychological tension, and graphic scenes that could be difficult to watch. It's not a light or easy viewing experience, so it's best suited for those who enjoy darker, more challenging narratives.
